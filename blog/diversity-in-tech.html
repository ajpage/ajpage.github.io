Diversity in Tech

January 24, 2016

Alyssa Page

Despite half of the US workforce being women, this does not extend to the tech world where it is dominated by a male population. Even more than this, minorities are even more underrepresented in tech. I acknowledge my white skin privilege in this regard. Diversity in tech continues to create buzz but still employment demographics continue to be heavily white and male. There is a lot of buzz without enough action behind it.

To create a more inclusive industry, there has to be an active push. A push to seriously recruit those people that are not included by default. A push to relentlessly provide space and mentoring and accessibility. I think this has to start pre-employment. Start with the youth and get them interested in tech, foster a sense of belonging and continue this to adulthood. There should be more women and people of color in leadership roles so that those working for them see they have a chance to grow and to belong. There are a lot of opportunities to demand inclusion, but there has to be action and not just talk.
